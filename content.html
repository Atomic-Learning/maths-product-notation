What product notation means

Product notation uses the capital pi symbol, \(\prod\), to represent repeated multiplication in a compact form. The general notation is:

$$\prod_{i=a}^{b} f(i)$$

This means: multiply the terms $f(a), f(a+1), \ldots, f(b)$.

Reading the notation

  • The index variable ($i$ in the above example) increases from the lower bound to the upper bound in steps of 1.
  • The lower bound ($a$ in the above example) is where the index starts.
  • The upper bound ($b$ in the example above) is where the index ends.
  • $f(i)$ is the term being multiplied at each step, which can be any function of the index.

Examples

$$\prod_{i=1}^{4} i = 1 \times 2 \times 3 \times 4 = 24$$

$$\prod_{k=2}^{5} (k+1) = 3 \times 4 \times 5 \times 6 = 360$$
